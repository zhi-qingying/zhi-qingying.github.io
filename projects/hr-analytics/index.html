📐 Key DAX Measures

Custom DAX formulas created in Power BI for dynamic calculations and interactive filtering.

// ============================================
// 1. Core Metrics
// ============================================

// Total Employees Count
Total Employees = COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data')

// Attrition Count (target = 1 means looking for new job)
Attrition Count = CALCULATE(
    COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data'),
    'cleaned_hr_data'[target] = 1
)

// Overall Attrition Rate (%)
Attrition Rate % = DIVIDE([Attrition Count], [Total Employees], 0) * 100


// ============================================
// 2. Risk Segmentation
// ============================================

// High Risk: Experience < 5 years AND City Dev Index < 0.8
High Risk Count = CALCULATE(
    [Total Employees],
    'cleaned_hr_data'[experience] < 5,
    'cleaned_hr_data'[city_development_index] < 0.8
)

High Risk Attrition Rate % = DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE(
        [Attrition Count],
        'cleaned_hr_data'[experience] < 5,
        'cleaned_hr_data'[city_development_index] < 0.8
    ),
    [High Risk Count],
    0
) * 100

// Low Risk: Experience >= 5 years AND City Dev Index >= 0.8
Low Risk Count = CALCULATE(
    [Total Employees],
    'cleaned_hr_data'[experience] >= 5,
    'cleaned_hr_data'[city_development_index] >= 0.8
)

Low Risk Attrition Rate % = DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE(
        [Attrition Count],
        'cleaned_hr_data'[experience] >= 5,
        'cleaned_hr_data'[city_development_index] >= 0.8
    ),
    [Low Risk Count],
    0
) * 100

// Risk Gap (difference between high and low risk groups)
Risk Gap = [High Risk Attrition Rate %] - [Low Risk Attrition Rate %]


// ============================================
// 3. Demographic Analysis
// ============================================

// Average City Development Index
Avg City Development Index = AVERAGE('cleaned_hr_data'[city_development_index])

// Attrition by Education (to be used with visual)
Attrition by Education = 
SUMMARIZE(
    'cleaned_hr_data',
    'cleaned_hr_data'[education_level],
    "Total", COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data'),
    "Attrition", CALCULATE(COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data'), 'cleaned_hr_data'[target] = 1),
    "Rate", DIVIDE(
        CALCULATE(COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data'), 'cleaned_hr_data'[target] = 1),
        COUNTROWS('cleaned_hr_data')
    ) * 100
)

💡 Key Business Insights

  • 📊 Overall Attrition Rate: 23% of candidates are actively seeking new employment opportunities
  • ⚠️ Highest Risk Group: Entry-level employees (0-2 years experience) show 32% attrition rate - 1.4x higher than average
  • 🎓 Education Impact: PhD holders have the lowest attrition (15%), while High School graduates show highest (28%)
  • 🏙️ City Development: Candidates in low-development cities are 2x more likely to seek job changes compared to high-development cities
  • 🏢 Company Size: Small companies (<50 employees) show 27% attrition vs 18% in large companies (10,000+ employees)
  • 📈 Risk Gap: 18% difference in attrition rate between high-risk and low-risk segments
  • 🔑 Top Predictors: City development index and total experience are the most important factors influencing job change decisions

🎯 Actionable Recommendations

  1. 🎯 Targeted Retention Programs: Implement specialized retention programs focusing on entry-level employees in low-development cities, as this segment shows the highest attrition risk (32%).
  2. 📈 Career Development Pathways: Establish clear career progression frameworks for employees with 0-5 years experience to reduce early-career turnover.
  3. 🏫 Education Partnerships: Partner with universities to offer continuous learning opportunities, particularly for employees without advanced degrees.
  4. 🏠 Remote Work Flexibility: Consider remote work policies for candidates in low-development areas to expand talent pool and improve retention.
  5. 💼 Competitive Compensation: Review compensation packages for small companies to remain competitive with larger organizations.
  6. 📊 Predictive Analytics Integration: Integrate the risk scoring model into HR systems for early identification of at-risk employees.
